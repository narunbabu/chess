# Chess99 Field Roles, Salary Plan, And Teaching Tools

Date: 2026-05-05

## 1. Team Structure Recommendation

Do not hire a large team immediately. Start with a controlled pilot team:

- 1 Growth/Operations Manager.
- 2 Business Developers for school partnerships.
- 1 Chess Program Coordinator.
- 4 Ambassadors for the first 2-3 weeks.
- Expand to 10 ambassadors only after conversion metrics are proven.
- Add 2-4 part-time chess coaches once paid batches start.

After 90 days, if the pilot works:

- 1 City Sales Lead.
- 4-6 Business Developers.
- 10-20 Ambassadors.
- 1 School Success Executive.
- 1 Tournament/Events Coordinator.
- 5-10 part-time coaches.

### Role 1: Business Development Executive - Schools

Purpose:

Acquire schools, book demos, close paid school programs, coordinate with the program team.

Responsibilities:

- Build school lead lists.
- Visit principals, coordinators, PE/activity heads.
- Present Chess99 school deck.
- Book demo days and competitions.
- Negotiate pilot/MOU/pricing.
- Coordinate onboarding with ambassadors/coaches.
- Track pipeline and weekly revenue.

Required profile:

- 1-4 years field sales or edtech/school sales.
- Telugu/Hindi/English speaking preferred for Telangana/AP.
- Comfortable entering schools and speaking to principals.
- Basic chess knowledge helpful but not mandatory.

Recommended pay:

- Fixed: INR 25,000-40,000/month in Hyderabad/Tier-1/strong Tier-2 markets.
- Travel/mobile: INR 5,000-8,000/month.
- Incentive: INR 2,000-5,000 per school demo completed, INR 10,000-25,000 per paid school closed depending on contract size, 2-5% of first-month school revenue.

KPIs:

- 20 new school contacts/week.
- 5 meetings/week.
- 2 demo bookings/week.
- 1 school event/week.
- 2 paid school conversions/month after ramp.

### Role 2: Senior Business Development / City Partnerships Lead

Purpose:

Own a city or district cluster and manage BDEs.

Responsibilities:

- Build district strategy.
- Close larger school chains.
- Handle principal/management negotiations.
- Manage BDE pipeline.
- Manage local partnerships with academies, apartment associations, and sponsors.

Recommended pay:

- Fixed: INR 50,000-80,000/month.
- Travel/mobile: INR 8,000-12,000/month.
- Incentive: 1-2% of collected school revenue plus team target bonus.

KPIs:

- 10 active schools/quarter.
- INR 3-8 lakh quarterly collected revenue after ramp.
- 70% school renewal from pilot to paid program.

### Role 3: Ambassador / Field Promoter

Purpose:

Canvass apartments, neighborhoods, school event exits, tuition centers; enroll users; ensure first game/puzzle; explain app utilities.

Responsibilities:

- QR enrollment.
- Parent/student pitch.
- First-game setup.
- WhatsApp follow-up.
- Report leads daily.
- Support school demo events.

Required profile:

- 18+.
- Local language fluency.
- Trustworthy, polite, presentable.
- Comfortable with smartphone and QR onboarding.
- Basic chess rules.

Recommended pay options:

Option A, part-time:

- Fixed stipend: INR 8,000-12,000/month.
- Travel/mobile: INR 2,000/month.
- Incentive: INR 10 per verified phone signup, INR 20 per first game/puzzle, INR 100-300 per paid conversion.

Option B, full-time:

- Fixed: INR 15,000-22,000/month.
- Travel/mobile: INR 3,000-5,000/month.
- Incentive: same as above, with weekly target bonus.

Why not pure commission:

Pure commission will push fake signups and bad behavior. Use low fixed plus verified-activity incentives.

KPIs:

- 25 meaningful conversations/day.
- 8 signups/day.
- 5 first activities/day.
- 2 qualified paid leads/day.
- Less than 20% invalid/fake/inactive signups.

### Role 4: Chess Program Coordinator

Purpose:

Ensure the school program actually runs well after sales.

Responsibilities:

- Design weekly activity plan.
- Coordinate coaches.
- Run competition logistics.
- Prepare school/parent reports.
- Train ambassadors on demo flow.
- Maintain student batches.

Recommended pay:

- Fixed: INR 30,000-50,000/month.
- Incentive: INR 2,000-5,000 per successfully completed school event or monthly retention bonus.

Required profile:

- Chess knowledge 1200+ playing strength or strong teaching exposure.
- Excellent coordination.
- Can speak to school staff and parents.

### Role 5: Chess Coach - Part Time

Purpose:

Teach paid batches and support competitions.

Recommended pay:

- Beginner coach: INR 250-500/hour.
- Strong local coach: INR 500-1,000/hour.
- FIDE-rated/titled coach: INR 1,000-3,000+/hour depending on profile.
- Monthly fixed for regular batches: INR 20,000-45,000/month part-time equivalent.

Salary.com's broad India chess coach benchmark is INR 34,998/month average, but actual market rates vary heavily by title, city, and online/offline format.

KPIs:

- Attendance retention above 75%.
- Homework completion above 50%.
- Monthly parent satisfaction above 4/5.
- Student improvement: games, puzzles, lesson completion, tournament participation.

### Role 6: School Success Executive

Purpose:

After a school signs up, keep it active.

Responsibilities:

- Weekly school report.
- Parent WhatsApp coordination.
- Renewals.
- Inactive student recovery.
- Certificate/tournament communication.

Recommended pay:

- Fixed: INR 25,000-40,000/month.
- Incentive: renewal bonus and retention bonus.

## 3. Compensation Model For Ambassadors

The existing Chess99 ambassador commission model in code offers milestone incentives and recurring commissions. For real-world field ambassadors, use two layers:

### Platform commission

Keep the built-in model:

- Signup with phone.
- First activity.
- 100 games/puzzles.
- Subscription revenue share.

### Field stipend

Add operational stipend:

- Attendance-based fixed pay.
- Travel/mobile reimbursements.
- Event-day bonus.
- Quality bonus for retained users.

Recommended anti-fraud rules:

- No payout for duplicate phone/device.
- No payout if user has no activity after 7 days.
- Higher payout only after parent phone verification.
- Paid conversion incentive only after successful payment and refund window.

## 4. Teaching Tools Needed Beyond Current App

### Must-have before serious school rollout

1. Teacher/class dashboard

Fields:

- School, branch, grade, section, roll number, parent phone, consent status.
- Student activity: games, puzzles, lessons, rating, last active.
- Filters for inactive students.

2. Assignment system

Capabilities:

- Assign lesson/puzzle/game to class.
- Due date.
- Completion view.
- Auto reminders.

3. Parent weekly report

Output:

- Student played X games.
- Solved Y puzzles.
- Completed Z lessons.
- Rating/progress.
- Next weekly target.
- Shareable WhatsApp image/PDF.

4. School event/tournament kit

Capabilities:

- Offline tournament entry.
- Pairing/standings printout.
- Manual result entry.
- Certificates.
- House/class/team leaderboard.

5. Child-safe account mode

Capabilities:

- Parent/guardian link.
- Restricted chat.
- No adult-child friend requests unless guardian-approved.
- Report/block.
- Teacher visibility.

6. Demo day mode

Capabilities:

- Fast guest/student onboarding.
- QR per school/event/ambassador.
- First game/puzzle checklist.
- Event conversion report.

### High-value teaching tools after pilot

1. Coach live board

- Coach broadcasts position.
- Students respond with move.
- Coach sees class answer distribution.

2. Puzzle race for classroom

- Teacher launches 5 puzzles.
- Live leaderboard.
- Individual reports.

3. CCT worksheet generator

- Position on left.
- Students write checks, captures, threats.
- App version mirrors the paper activity.

4. Placement test

- 10-15 questions/puzzles.
- Assigns Newcomer/Beginner/Improving Beginner/Club/Advanced path.

5. Certificates and badges

- First Game.
- 7-Day Streak.
- CCT Scanner.
- School Champion.
- Level Completion.

6. Curriculum packs

- 12-week beginner.
- 24-week club.
- 48-week competitive.
- Adult beginner 8-week.
- Parent-child weekend pack.

7. Multilingual content

- Telugu first for local launch.
- Hindi next.
- English always.

8. Coach annotation/review

- Coach can annotate student's game.
- Student receives 3 improvement points.
- Parent sees a simple summary.

## 6. Suggested Hiring Sequence

### Month 1

- Hire 1 Growth/Operations Manager.
- Hire 2 BDEs.
- Hire 4 ambassadors.
- Contract 1-2 part-time coaches.

### Month 2

- Expand ambassadors to 10 if signup quality is good.
- Add 1 program coordinator if school events exceed 2/week.

### Month 3

- Add school success executive if 5+ active schools.
- Add more coaches only after paid batch demand.

## 7. Interview Scorecards

### BDE scorecard

- School sales experience: 25%.
- Communication and confidence: 25%.
- Follow-up discipline: 20%.
- Local network: 15%.
- Chess/education empathy: 15%.

Practical task:

- Ask them to pitch Chess99 to a school principal in 3 minutes.

### Ambassador scorecard

- Trust and presentation: 30%.
- Local language communication: 25%.
- Smartphone/QR comfort: 20%.
- Basic chess knowledge: 15%.
- Reporting discipline: 10%.

Practical task:

- Ask them to onboard a mock parent and get one game started.

### Coach scorecard

- Chess understanding: 25%.
- Teaching clarity for children: 30%.
- Patience/classroom management: 20%.
- Homework/report discipline: 15%.
- Parent communication: 10%.

Practical task:

- Teach "fork" to a 7-year-old beginner in 5 minutes.
