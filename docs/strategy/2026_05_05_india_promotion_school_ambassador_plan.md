# Chess99 India Promotion Plan

Date: 2026-05-05

## 1. Market Entry Strategy

Start in one geography, preferably Hyderabad/Telangana and nearby AP districts, because Chess99 has local company presence and India-specific village/mandal/location logic already exists in the backend. Do not start by trying to win all India. Win 10 schools, 1,000 active students, and 100 paying families in one cluster first.

Primary market promise:

"Chess99 helps schools run chess activities and competitions, while students continue guided practice at home."

Secondary parent promise:

"Your child will play, solve, and improve every week. You can see the activity."

Avoid selling chess as only "become a champion". Sell three ladders:

1. Brain development and focus for all children.
2. School competition and confidence for active children.
3. Serious tournament pathway for talented children.

## 2. Target Segments

### Segment A: Private schools, grades 1-10

Best starting segment. They have activity budgets, parent WhatsApp groups, computer labs, annual day/event culture, and willingness to host competitions.

Pitch:

- Free chess awareness/demo day.
- Inter-house chess competition.
- School leaderboard.
- After-school chess club.
- Parent-visible practice at home.

### Segment B: Apartment communities and gated colonies

Useful for ambassadors. Easier than schools because decisions are faster, but conversion sizes are smaller.

Pitch:

- Sunday chess camp.
- Apartment kids tournament.
- First game and QR enrollment.
- Parent WhatsApp follow-up.

### Segment C: Coaching centers and local chess coaches

Treat them as partners, not competitors.

Pitch:

- Use Chess99 to assign puzzles, track practice, conduct internal tournaments, and earn referral commission.

### Segment D: Adults

Adults should be a separate weekend/evening product:

- "Learn chess with your child."
- "Corporate chess thinking workshop."
- "Beginner to club player in 12 weeks."

Do not mix adult messaging with school-child acquisition.

## 3. Offers To Take To Schools

### Offer 1: Free Chess Thinking Day

Purpose: lead generation and credibility.

Format:

- 10-minute principal/management briefing.
- 20-minute assembly/demo: "How chess trains attention and decision making."
- 30-minute computer lab or classroom QR onboarding.
- Every student plays one game or solves first puzzle.
- Collect parent consent/contact through school-approved channel.
- Give school a one-page report: students onboarded, games played, top performers.

Conversion target:

- 20-40% of participating students create account.
- 5-15% convert to paid within 14 days if follow-up is strong.

### Offer 2: Inter-House / Inter-Class Chess99 Cup

Purpose: make Chess99 visible and social.

Format:

- School provides houses/classes.
- Chess99 runs online qualifiers or offline board rounds.
- Finals can be projected in auditorium.
- Certificates for participation, top 10, best girl, youngest player, top house/class.
- Parent WhatsApp sharing after event.

Commercial hook:

- Free or low-cost event for first school.
- Paid package for recurring monthly/quarterly competition.

### Offer 3: After-School Chess Club

Purpose: recurring revenue.

Suggested pricing:

- School group: INR 999-1,999 per student/month depending on coach involvement.
- App-only school license: INR 99-199 per student/month if school already has a teacher/coach.
- Premium coached batch: INR 2,499-4,999 per student/month for small groups.

Structure:

- 1 live class/week.
- 5 assigned puzzles/week.
- 1 game/week.
- 1 monthly tournament.
- Parent progress summary.

### Offer 4: School Annual Chess League

Purpose: long-term retention.

Structure:

- School registration.
- Students grouped by grade and level.
- Monthly leaderboard.
- Quarterly tournament.
- Annual school champion.
- Inter-school finals if multiple schools join.

This is the best long-term differentiator because Chess99 already has championship and organization features.

## 4. Ambassador Home Enrollment Plan

You mentioned roughly 10 ambassadors who will canvass homes, enroll students, get one game played, and explain training utilities. That can work only if the process is strict and measurable.

### Ambassador daily workflow

1. Visit assigned apartments/colonies/school exits/tuition centers with permission.
2. Show a 60-second pitch to parent/student.
3. Scan QR or open short referral URL.
4. Create account or capture parent phone for callback.
5. Student plays one quick game or solves one puzzle.
6. Ambassador marks status: new signup, first game done, parent interested, paid interest, callback required.
7. Send WhatsApp follow-up with the student's link and next task.

### Ambassador kit

- Chess99 ID card.
- Police/background verification copy or company verification letter.
- QR poster.
- A5 handout for parents.
- Phone with data pack.
- Demo login and demo child account.
- Portable chessboard.
- 3-minute pitch in English, Telugu, and Hindi.
- Objection handling sheet.
- Google Form/CRM form for lead capture.

### Ambassador KPIs

Daily:

- 25 meaningful conversations.
- 12 QR scans.
- 8 signups.
- 5 first games/puzzles completed.
- 2 parent follow-up calls booked.

Weekly per ambassador:

- 150 conversations.
- 50-70 signups.
- 25-35 first activities.
- 5-10 paid conversions or trial class bookings.

For 10 ambassadors:

- 500-700 signups/week is possible on paper, but expect quality to vary.
- Strong reality target: 200-300 useful signups/week and 25-50 paid leads/week in early weeks.

## 5. Business Developer School Sales Plan

Business developers should not behave like generic app salespeople. Their job is school relationship building, event setup, and conversion.

### Weekly school BD workflow

1. Build list of 50 schools in one district/zone.
2. Call/cold visit 20.
3. Secure 5 principal/activity-coordinator meetings.
4. Book 2 demo events.
5. Run 1 event with an operations/coach person.
6. Close one of: paid club, competition package, app-only school license, pilot MOU.

### School sales pitch structure

Opening:

"We run chess activity programs for schools. We do the demo, competition, student onboarding, and home practice tracking."

Problem:

"Schools want chess because it improves focus and thinking, but they do not always have a coach, tournament system, or way to keep children practicing at home."

Chess99 solution:

"We provide the platform, QR onboarding, tactical practice, competitions, progress data, and optional coaches."

Close:

"Let us run one Chess Thinking Day for grades 3-8. You will get a report and can decide whether to start a school chess club."

## 6. 90-Day Rollout

### Days 1-15: Preparation

- Create school pitch deck.
- Create parent one-pager.
- Create safety policy.
- Create ambassador SOP and script.
- Prepare demo accounts and QR posters.
- Choose 2 zones in Hyderabad or nearby.
- Recruit 2 business developers and 4 ambassadors first, not all 10.
- Build CRM pipeline with lead stages.

Success criteria:

- 100 target schools listed.
- 30 principal/activity-coordinator contacts.
- 10 meetings booked.
- 4 demo days booked.

### Days 16-45: Pilot

- Run 4-6 school demo days.
- Run 2 apartment tournaments.
- Recruit remaining ambassadors only after scripts work.
- Collect testimonials and photos/videos with consent.
- Measure conversion by school/source/referral code.

Success criteria:

- 1,000 students reached.
- 300 accounts created.
- 150 first games/puzzles completed.
- 30 paid conversions or trial class bookings.
- 2 schools agree to paid club or recurring event.

### Days 46-90: Scale Cluster

- Expand to 10-15 schools.
- Start monthly Chess99 School League.
- Launch Sunday apartment chess camps.
- Publish first case study.
- Add school admin reporting/certificate workflow if not already built.

Success criteria:

- 10 schools activated.
- 2,500 students reached.
- 800 accounts.
- 400 first activities.
- 100 paid students or equivalent school license revenue.
- 1 public inter-school event.

## 9. Risks And Controls

Risk: Ambassadors produce fake or low-quality signups.

Control: Pay meaningful incentives only after verified phone, first activity, and retained activity after 7 days.

Risk: Schools hesitate due to child safety.

Control: Prepare safety policy, restricted child accounts, verified staff, parent consent, no unsafe chat.

Risk: Product feels weaker than free Lichess.

Control: Sell school service, local reports, competitions, and guided learning, not only app features.

Risk: BD team overpromises coaching outcomes.

Control: Use approved scripts and avoid guarantees like FIDE rating in X months.

Risk: Home visits create trust concerns.

Control: ID cards, prior apartment association permission, parent-only communication, no one-on-one unsupervised child interaction.
