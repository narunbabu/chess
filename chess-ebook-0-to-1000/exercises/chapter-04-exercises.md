## Answers

**4.1:** 1.e4, 2.Nf3, 3.Bc4, 4.c3, 5.d4, 6.cxd4 (after ...exd4), 7.Nc3 or O-O.
**4.2:** 1=Control center, 2=Develop pieces, 3=Castle, 4=Connect rooks.
**4.3:** A=White gains a tempo — Nc3 develops with attack on the queen. B=No, White recaptures with a pawn that also controls the center (cxd4 opens c-file and maintains d4 pawn). It's a developing recapture.
**4.4:** White threatens Qxf7#. Black should play 3... g6 (attack queen, gain tempo) or 3... Qe7. After 3... Nf6??, 4. Qxf7# is Scholar's Mate.
**4.5:** Trap 1=Legal's Mate: 6. Bxf7+ Ke7 7. Nd5# (or similar). Trap 2=Queen's Gambit Accepted trap: 6. Qf3! attacking the rook on a8 (Qxa8). Black loses a rook.
**4.6:** A=Kingside (O-O) — it's ready, only 2 pieces to move. B=Don't castle yet or Kingside — queenside exposes king on open e-file. C=Queenside (O-O-O) — pieces are ready, king is safer toward queenside if enemy is castled kingside and White wants to attack on the kingside.
**4.7:** Personal choices. Recommended: vs 1...e5=Italian (3.Bc4), vs 1...e6=Nc3 French, vs 1...c5=Open Sicilian (2.Nf3, 3.d4), vs 1...c6=Classical (2.d4, 3.Nc3), vs 1...d5=exd5. As Black: vs e4=1...e5 or 1...d5 (Scandinavian), vs d4=1...d5, vs Nf3=1...d5, vs c4=1...e5.
