---

## Answers

**2.1:** 1.e4=Good, 1.d4=Good, 1.h4=Bad, 1.Nf3=Good, 1.a3=Bad, 1.c4=Good, 1.Na3=Bad, 1.f3=Bad
**2.2:** White=4 (Nf3, Bc4, O-O, d3 doesn't develop a piece but supports center). Black=4 (Nc6, Bc5, Nf6, O-O). Both have developed well.
**2.3:** A=Fork (knight forks king, queen, rook), B=Pin (knight is pinned to king), C=Skewer (queen behind king on same file), D=Discovered Attack (or Discovered Check if Ng5 gives check)
**2.4:** White attackers=3 (pawn on d4, knight on f3, bishop on c4). Black defenders=2 (knight on c6, queen on d8 — pawn on e5 defends itself). 3>2, so White can win the pawn if they capture with the least valuable piece first (d4xe5).
**2.5:** White threatens Qxf7# (Scholar's Mate). Three responses: 2... Nc6 (develop, defend e5), 2... Qe7 (defend e5 and f7), 2... Qf6 (defend f7). Nc6 also develops a piece.
**2.6:** Seq1=2...f6 is the Damiano Defense — weakens king, blocks knight, and White can play Nxe5! Seq2=6...Bc5 is OK, Black has developed well and can castle next. Seq3=White's queen on d4 is centralized and Black has no easy way to attack it (White can play Nc3, Bd3, etc. developing with tempo).
