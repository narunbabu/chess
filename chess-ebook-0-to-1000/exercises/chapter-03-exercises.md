## Answers

**3.1:** Pos1 (White to move): Checks=Qh5+? (no, blocked). Captures=Nxe5, Bxf7+ (check+capture). Threats=Nxe5 (attacks Qd8 via discovered B), d4 (attacks e5 twice). Black: Checks=Qh4+? (no). Captures=Nxe4. Threats=Bc5 (attack f2). Pos2 (Black): Checks=Qxf2#, Qh2#, Qh1#. Captures=Qxf2, Nxd5. Threats=Qh2#. Fork=Qxf2+ forks the king and rook!

**3.2:** 1.Nc3xd5, 2.Nf6 or Qd8, 3.Bg2xd5, 4.the remaining piece, 5.c4xd5. White wins a pawn (3 attackers vs 2 defenders).

**3.3:** Bxf6! removes the defender of d5. After ...Qxf6 (or gxf6 or something), White plays Bxd5 (if Bishop is on c4) or just captures the now-undefended d5 pawn. White wins a pawn.

**3.4:** ...Qd8-a5+! (checking the White king). After White blocks or moves the king, THEN Black recaptures on c6. Black gains a tempo and possibly wins material.

**3.5:** 1. Bb4 pins Nf3 to the queen on d1 (or king on e1 — depends on exact position). 2. Nd4 is NOT good because the knight is pinned — it cannot legally move! 3. Play c3 to break the pin, or Bd2 to block, or O-O to protect the king.
