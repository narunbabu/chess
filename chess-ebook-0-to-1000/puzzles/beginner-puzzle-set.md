# Beginner Puzzle Set: 20 Essential Positions for ELO 0-1000

These puzzles reinforce the concepts from all six chapters. Set up each position on a board (or use the Chess99 Tactical Trainer) and solve.

---

## Puzzle 1: The Fork (Chapter 2 & 3)
**FEN:** r1bqkb1r/pppp1ppp/2n5/4N3/4P3/8/PPPP1PPP/RNBQKB1R b KQkq - 0 3
**Theme:** Knight Fork
**Question:** White's knight on e5 forks Black's king (g8), queen (d8), and rook (a8). What should Black have done to prevent this?
**Hint:** Don't let the knight reach e5.

## Puzzle 2: Back Rank Mate (Chapter 2 & 6)

**Theme:** Back Rank Mate
**Question:** White to move. How does White deliver checkmate in 1 move?
**Answer:** Re8#

## Puzzle 3: The Pin (Chapter 2 & 3)
**FEN:** r1bqk2r/pppp1ppp/2n2n2/4p3/1b2P3/2NP1N2/PPP2PPP/R1BQKB1R b KQkq - 0 5
**Theme:** Pin
**Question:** Black's bishop on b4 pins the knight on c3. What is pinned to what? How can White break the pin?
**Hint:** White's knight can't move because the king is behind it. Play a3 or Bd2.

---

## Puzzle 4: Queen + King Checkmate (Chapter 1 & 5)
**FEN:** 8/8/8/5k2/8/8/8/4Q1K1 w - - 0 1
**Theme:** K+Q vs K
**Question:** White to move. Deliver checkmate against the lone Black king. Can you do it in 5 moves or fewer?
**Hint:** Cut off the king first, then bring your king.

## Puzzle 5: Scholar's Mate (Chapter 4)
**FEN:** r1bqkb1r/pppp1ppp/2n2n2/4p2Q/2B1P3/8/PPPP1PPP/RNB1K1NR w KQkq - 0 4
**Theme:** Scholar's Mate
**Question:** White (who has already played e4, Qh5, Bc4) sees Black has played ...Nf6. What should White play?
**Answer:** Qxf7#

## Puzzle 6: Defending Scholar's Mate (Chapter 4)
**FEN:** r1bqkb1r/pppp1ppp/2n5/4p2Q/2B1P3/8/PPPP1PPP/RNB1K1NR b KQkq - 0 3
**Theme:** Defense
**Question:** Black to move. White is threatening Qxf7#. What should Black play to defend?
**Hint:** Three good moves: ...Qe7, ...Qf6, or ...g6.

## Puzzle 7: Removal of the Defender (Chapter 3)
**FEN:** r1bqkb1r/ppp2ppp/2n2n2/3pp3/2B1P3/3P1N2/PPP2PPP/RNBQK2R w KQkq - 0 5
**Theme:** Removing the Defender / Undermining
**Question:** White to move. Black's knight on f6 is the only defender of the pawn on d5. How can White exploit this?
**Answer:** Bg5! (pinning the defender). After ...Be7 or ..., White captures on d5.

## Puzzle 8: Discovered Attack (Chapter 3)
**FEN:** r1bqk2r/pppp1ppp/2n5/4N3/1bB1P3/8/PPPP1PPP/RNBQK2R w KQkq - 0 5
**Theme:** Discovered Attack
**Question:** White to move. White's knight on e5 and bishop on c4 form a battery. What does White's knight threaten, and what does moving it discover?
**Hint:** Moving the knight discovers an attack from the bishop on f7.

---

## Puzzle 9: Counting Attackers (Chapter 2)
**FEN:** r1bqk2r/ppp2ppp/2n2n2/3pp3/1bBPP3/2N2N2/PPP2PPP/R1BQK2R w KQkq - 0 7
**Theme:** Counting Attackers vs Defenders
**Question:** White wants to capture on e5. Count the attackers and defenders. Is it safe?
**Answer:** White has 3 attackers (Nf3, d4 pawn, Bc1 or Nc3? depends). Black has 2-3 defenders. White can capture after ...exd4 Nxd4.

---

## Puzzle 10: Zwischenzug (Chapter 3)
**FEN:** r1bqk2r/pppp1ppp/2n5/4p3/1bB1P3/2N2N2/PPPP1PPP/R1BQK2R w KQkq - 0 6
**Theme:** Zwischenzug (In-Between Move)
**Question:** Black's bishop on b4 is attacked by White's knight on c3. Black could retreat. But what in-between move does Black have first?
**Answer:** ...Bxc3+! (check first). After White recaptures, then Black can move another piece. Or ...Qa5+.

---

## Puzzle 11: Passed Pawn Breakthrough (Chapter 5)
**FEN:** 8/ppp5/8/PPP5/8/8/8/8 w - - 0 1
**Theme:** Pawn Breakthrough
**Question:** White to move. White has three connected pawns facing Black's three pawns. How can White create a passed pawn?
**Answer:** 1. b6! axb6 (or cxb6) 2. a6! (or c6) and one of the pawns promotes.

---

## Puzzle 12: Opposition (Chapter 5)
**FEN:** 8/8/8/4k3/8/3K4/8/8 w - - 0 1
**Theme:** Opposition
**Question:** White to move. Who has the opposition? What's White's best move?
**Answer:** Black has the opposition (they don't have to move -- wait, White to move means Black has opposition). White should play Ke3! (taking opposition when Black can't maintain it -- actually this is tricky. Kd3-e3? No, 1. Ke3! Kd5 (or Ke6), and now White has the opposition.)

## Puzzle 13: Basic Fork (Chapter 2)
**FEN:** r2qk2r/ppp2ppp/2n5/4N3/2B1P1b1/8/PPPP1PPP/R1BQK2R w KQkq - 0 6
**Theme:** Knight Fork
**Question:** White's knight on e5 is attacked by Black's bishop on g4. Should White move the knight? If so, where?
**Hint:** Nxf7! forks queen and rook. After Kxf7, Bxf7+ and White wins material.

---

## Puzzle 14: The Skewer (Chapter 2 & 5)
**FEN:** 4k3/4r3/8/8/8/8/8/2R1K3 w - - 0 1
**Theme:** Skewer
**Question:** White rook on c1, Black rook on e7, Black king on e8. What's the skewer?
**Answer:** Rc8+! The king must move, and then Rxe7 wins the rook.

---

## Puzzle 15: Avoiding Stalemate (Chapter 1 & 5)
**FEN:** 8/8/8/2K5/8/4Q3/8/7k w - - 0 1
**Theme:** Stalemate Awareness
**Question:** White to move. White has K+Q vs lone Black king on h1. What moves would be STALEMATE?
**Answer:** Qf2? (or Qg3? or Qe2?) -- the king on h1 has no squares but is NOT in check.

---

## Puzzle 16: Double Check (Chapter 3)
**FEN:** r1bq1rk1/pppp1ppp/2n5/4N3/1bB1P3/8/PPPP1PPP/RNBQK2R w KQkq - 0 5
**Theme:** Discovered Double Check (hypothetical)
**Question:** White: N on e5, B on c4. Black: K on g8. White plays Nf7! What type of check is delivered?
**Hint:** It would be double check if both the knight and bishop give check. But Nf7 from e5 doesn't check from c4. Actually, Nxg6+! (if pawn on h7) -- discovered check from the bishop AND check from the knight. Double check! The king must move.

---

## Puzzle 17: Development vs. Material (Chapter 4)
**FEN:** rnbqkbnr/pppp1ppp/8/4p3/4P3/8/PPPP1PPP/RNBQKBNR w KQkq - 0 2
**Theme:** Opening Choice
**Question:** It's White's second move. What are the top 3 developing moves?
**Answer:** 2. Nf3 (develop knight, attack e5), 2. Nc3 (develop knight, control center), 2. Bc4 (develop bishop, target f7), 2. d4 (strike center immediately).

---

## Puzzle 18: Knight on the Rim (Chapter 3)
**FEN:** r1bqkbnr/pppp1ppp/2n5/4p3/4P3/5N2/PPPP1PPP/RNBQKB1R w KQkq - 0 3
**Theme:** Trapping Pieces
**Question:** Should White play Na3 or Nc3? Why?
**Answer:** Nc3 -- it's more central. Na3 ("knight on the rim is dim") controls only 4 squares. Nc3 controls 8 and fights for the center.

---

## Puzzle 19: King + Rook Checkmate (Chapter 5)
**FEN:** 8/8/8/8/3k4/8/8/4K2R w - - 0 1
**Theme:** K+R vs K
**Question:** White to move. Checkmate the lone Black king. Can you do it in 10 moves or fewer?
**Hint:** Use the rook to cut off the king (to one edge of the board), then bring your king.

---

## Puzzle 20: Simplify to Win (Chapter 5)
**FEN:** r1b2rk1/ppp2ppp/2n5/4p1q1/2B1P3/2NP4/PPP2PPP/R2QK2R w KQ - 0 8
**Theme:** Trading to Simplify
**Question:** White is up a bishop but Black has an active queen. What should White offer to trade?
**Answer:** Offer a queen trade (Qd1-g4 or Qd1-h5 attacking the queen). If Black trades, White's material advantage becomes decisive. If Black avoids, their queen has to retreat and loses activity.
